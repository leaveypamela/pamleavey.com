How is your artwork born?

If the portal is where the journey begins, the ritual is the threshold where it takes shape.

I begin by opening the channel. I sit in ritual with your name and date of birth, draw tarot to meet your spiritual guides, and invite the five elements to speak through the work.

No photo. No history. Only presence, intention and trust. The colours arrive, the symbols emerge, and the painting takes form over weeks of quiet, intuitive work in London.

When it is complete, your artwork arrives with a full written soul reading in PDF, explaining the meanings, colours, forms and channelled messages, along with channelled affirmations and a certificate of authenticity.

The reading is my perspective, what came through for me as I painted. It is an opening, not a verdict. You are welcome to see what I saw, and to keep discovering your own meanings over time. The piece stays with you, and so does the unfolding.

The Artist

I am Pam.

Soul Artist, medium and intuitive painter based in London. Born in Brazil, living between two worlds, I have always been drawn to what lies beneath the surface of things.

I spent 11 years as a tattoo artist, working intimately with people and learning how much the soul carries in silence. That work taught me to listen with my hands, to feel what someone could not yet put into words, and to trust what arrived through the creative act.

A background in architecture gave me an eye for form, structure and symbol. An encounter with Jungian depth psychology gave me the language for what I had always been sensing. And a lifelong spiritual practice, rooted in mediumship, tarot and Afro-Brazilian tradition, gave me the channel through which it all flows.

Today I paint souls. I am a Cancerian, Kin 243 Blue Solar Night of the Eagle Wavespell. I work with tarot, crystals, candles, white sage and water as ritual tools to open the channel before each piece begins. My guides, The Tower, The Devil and Judgement, hold the space: transformation, shadow and the call of the soul.

I do not paint what I see. I paint what arrives.
